THE FINAL PRODUCT:

Genda app aims to help the user

Problem Statement

Current planner apps lack the customizability and flexibility that people need, leading to the planning experience being tedious, confusing, or spread throughout many different avenues.

Solution Statement

Genda aims to condense the planning process, allowing full customization so users can be as thorough or simplistic as their planning needs.

User Research

The Goal:

As user researchers, we want to understand how users organize their task throughout the day and if there are any methods to streamline the process.

Our Objectives:

● What aspects of the users lifestyle requires organization and planning?

● What is the user's experience using planning apps and tools?

● In the user's experiences, what are some aspects of planning that they enjoy or cause frustration?

The Results

● Many of those we interviewed felt that planning was overwhelming, from difficulty navigating planning apps and it being tedious to fill out many required fields for a simple reminder note.

● Those who use traditional pen and paper planners were hesitant to move to a digital planner because they lack the customization of handwritten notebooks.

User Persona

From our interviews and research, the team created a fictionalized user named Nana Kurosawa. Nana is a 35 year old CPA that wants to strike a better work life balance than what she actually has.

With Nana embodying our research, we knew who we were designing for.

Competitor Analysis

The competitor analysis reflects the competition that is currently out on the market. The analysis also revealed how people would benefit and enjoy the ability to customize as well as have easy navigation.
